Skills

I focus on incorporting the benefits of emerging technology into the accounting field.

The days where accountants labored over massive spreadsheets are long gone. Today, accountants must be able to incorporate emerging technology to support their audits in ways that were not possible beforehand. I believe that mastering these technologies is the key to success for the 21st century accountant, and I have made it my goal to be proficient in as many as possible.

  • Data Analytics

    The size of data today requires accountants to use analytical tools to gain insights. Using technologies such as Alteryx, Python, or VBA, I can perform more advanced analytics on data than possible with simple Excel formulas.

  • Data Visualization

    Expressing insights from data is also critical. Using technologies like Microsoft PowerBI and Tableau, I can create interactive visualizations that take tons of data and expresses it in a way that is clear and understandable to the team and the client.

  • Automation

    Holding back many accountants are easily automatable low-level tasks that take up valuable time. Using technologies like VBA or Python, I can script simple programs to replace oft-repeated tasks. With my experience with robotics process automation (RPA) tools like Microsoft Power Automate or UIPath, I can prepare more complex automations to replace entire processes.

  • Accounting Technology

    With my real-world experience, I am skilled in accounting tools that make up the bulk of accounting work. Whether it is simply Microsoft Excel or software like SQL, SAP, or IDEA, I can easily complete any task no matter how challenging.

  • Communication

    Even though emerging technology is critical to an accountant, communication is just as important. I have plenty of experience working in successful teams, whether it be for an audit client or a student-led startup.
